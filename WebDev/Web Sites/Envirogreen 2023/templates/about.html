History of Envirogreen School of Technology

"I started school from family leisure then it became a training school thru TESDA... then a high school, and now college. By God's grace and strong determination, we made these things possible..."

Engr. Marcelina G. Supsup

EVEFI President


It all began with a simple but earnest desire to help family, communities, and the future generation.

The Envirogreen Educational Village Foundation, Inc. was inaugurated on August 18, 2017. It began as a school that offers TESDA programs in light of the agency's mandate to empower and make people and communities productive through excellent training programs and services. As a TESDA partner institution, within the first three years of its establishment, EVEFI was accredited to offer the following programs by the issuance of Certification of TVET Program Registration (CTPR):


Organic Agriculture Production NCII

Pest Management (Vegetables) NCII

Agro-entrepreneurship NCII

It is worth mentioning that the school had already trained 1,650 Senior High School students from 47 schools in Region XII and some areas in Region XI through the Joint Delivery Voucher Program (JDVP) initiative of the Department of Education (DepEd).

In 2020, EVEFI applied for government permits to offer the Senior High School and Bachelor of Agricultural Technology Programs from both the Department of Education (DepEd), and the Commission on Higher Education (CHED). There are two tracks offered for the Senior High School Program namely the Academic and TVL tracks. For the Academic track, two strands are offered, the Accounting, Business, and Management strand, and the Humanities and Social Sciences strand. For the TVL track, on the other hand, Agri-Fishery Arts, and Home Economics strands. For the Agri Fishery Arts, Agricultural Crops Production NCII and Pest Management (Vegetables) NCII are offered while Local Guiding Services NCII and Tourism Management NCII are offered to students.

Additionally, along with the institution’s application for government permits for its B.A.T. program, the school was registered to do business under the name Envirogreen School of Technology.

In September 2022, Envirogreen was granted temporary permit to offer 2nd level Bachelor of Science in Agriculture (BSA) in lieu of the BAT program which was abolished by the CHED. Two more programs are also offered by the school namely the Bachelor of Management Accounting (BSMA) and Bachelor of Technical-Vocational Teacher Education (BTVTEd).

Envirogreen was not only a school that offers TESDA Scholarship Programs. It was, and still is, TESDA’s partner in developing and honing marketable skills among potential scholars from the communities within Region XII.
